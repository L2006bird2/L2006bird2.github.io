Imbrem Noctem

It would be remiss to merely label them as cicadas. They do not evoke the graceful dance of butterflies or adhere to the predictable patterns of the common insects that linger in the shadowy corners of our consciousness. Rather, they unfailingly provoke within us a sense of uncertainty as they slumber amidst the night's shadows or alight upon our curtains. They have become symbolic of rebirth and resurrection, a profound significance for such diminutive creatures. A world bereft of cicadas would be a world devoid of resurgence, and consequently, a world without perpetuity, a revelation that crystallized for me on that day.

Nosce te ipsum

Weary. So, weary. The entire castle slept as the guards crept along the dreary halls. Their eyes focused on the outer walls and the ancient ruins they protected. I delicately, carefully, ever so cautiously, traced my way from the abhorrent chambers and towards the winding halls of the undisturbed and unchanged library. My single exposed eye wept at the marvelous sight before me. From ceiling to floor lay shelves upon shelves of literature that seemingly breathed life into the frigid marble pillars. The engravings of my mask caught the moonlight and filtered through the window like dozens of beautiful birds. I wept with joy, for I was free.

"Write a brief introduction to yourself!"

My name is Sammy, and I’m currently a student in your Writers Craft course. What makes a good writer? It’s a question I’ve perpetually asked myself ever since I penned my first short story at 9. Throughout my writing journey, my work has always been influenced by literary figures such as Virginia Woolf, Shakespeare, Edgar Allan Poe, and Charles Bukowski. I find resonance in their profound exploration of the darker side of human nature and emotions through written expression. My favourite subjects include English, mathematics, and physics. Due to my passion towrds these subjects, I aim to pursue computer science or mathematics during post secondary. In this class, I hope to discover a platform to channel my passion for writing, particularly in the realms of short stories and poetry.

"Identify an interest that you would like to describe in greater detail."

Within the vast landscape of literature and the different themes and genres that make it up, my interest lies in crafting poetry and short fictional stories that delve into the intricacies of common day to day characters and explore the darker themes that surround them. This fascination stems from my belief that by exploring the darker aspects of humanity, we gain a deeper understanding of the intricate interplay between the good and bad that shapes the human experience. This realm of literature is crucial and can benefit any student writing or reading it because, in order to truly understand the deepest parts of human psychology, we must delve deeper into the intricacies that reside within the shadows of our narratives. Exploring the darker aspects of human experience allows for a nuanced examination of emotions, struggles, and the complexities that shape our perception of the world. By venturing into these uncharted territories, we gain profound insights that not only enrich our understanding of literature but also provide a mirror to the intricacies of the human psyche.

"What’s one thing you don’t like?"

One thing I dislike is having to eat overly salty food. Salty food is the most disturbingly vapid, and internally confused edible train wreck I’ve ever had the deeply sorrowful displeasure of allowing to pass through my gullet. As a fan of sweet, spicy, cold, and savory food, I despise any food that contains copious amounts of salt.

"Describe something you find challenging"

One aspect of chemistry that I find particularly challenging is the topic of chemical bonding and molecular interactions, especially in the first part of the subject. However, I have a strong affinity (pun intended!) for stoichiometry and equilibrium chemistry because of their mathematical and analytical components. Given my interest in mathematics and logical reasoning, I find these units more comprehensible and engaging. Chemical bonding, on the other hand, tends to be more intricate compared to other areas of chemistry, such as intermolecular attractions between molecules. Overall, I find equilibrium and stoichiometry to be much more intriguing. Nonetheless, I am committed to enhancing my skills in all aspects of chemistry through dedicated practice, as I believe that practice leads to improvement.
